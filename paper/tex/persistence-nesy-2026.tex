\begin{abstract}
We present \textbf{Persistence}, a neurosymbolic substrate where symbolic state --- bitemporal datoms, content-addressed plan ASTs, policy-as-data, and boundary specs --- carries formal properties on top of which neural agency composes. The central invariant: every piece of agent state (memory, audit, plan, skill, transaction) is an immutable, content-addressed, bitemporal datom; effects route through a composable handler stack whose entries are themselves datoms; plans are EDN ASTs stored as Merkle-DAGs; counterfactual replay is a first-class query over the log. Contemporary frameworks fragment auditability, counterfactuals, and controllable learning across incompatible substrates~\citep{kurtic2025zep,cheng2025pangolin,khattab2023dspy,wang2023voyager,anon2026camo}; Persistence's claim is that these properties can be \emph{derived} from a single substrate rather than re-engineered per system. The reference implementation v0.1.0a1 + v0.4.0a1 + v0.5.1 ships six of seven modules (\textbf{931 tests green, 7 xfailed}): Fact, Effect, Spec, Replay, Txn (snapshot-isolated STM with intent-log read-set provenance and \C{:effect/txn-commit} threaded into the audit chain), and a minimal Plan (parse / unparse / walk / spec-validation, 128-bit content-addressing). Plan execution (MIPROv2/MCTS, 4-gate skill promotion) and REPL remain Phase 2.C / 2.E. We state five propositions and machine-check them on the shipped artifact; the headline two: (i) any handler stack is well-formed iff every catalog operation is covered --- checkable in linear time in stack size for fixed catalog by \C{Runtime.is\_well\_formed}; (ii) NO-OP \C{replay(T, I)} yields a canonical-hash byte-identical to the factual trajectory's --- a structural determinism guarantee stronger than CAMO's statistical seed-replay \emph{in the fully-instrumented NO-OP setting} (toy agents with recorded per-step rng-consumption); we do not claim it outside. \textbf{Status legend:} \textbf{S} = shipped (Phase~1 / 2.B / 2.D); \textbf{P} = parse / spec layer only; \textbf{D} = designed. Proposition statements are unchanged since v0.7; machine-checked validations rerun green on v0.5.1. A Phase-1 microbench (\sectionref{sec:eval-microbench}) ships at submission time; LongMemEval, CAMO distributional fidelity, a 50-trajectory regulator-replay, and a production case study are staged for 2026-07-20 camera-ready and labelled [TBD] honestly.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}

\textbf{Research question.} When neural agency is scaffolded by a symbolic substrate, what formal properties does the combined system inherit that neither layer provides alone? We argue that six substrate invariants --- immutable bitemporal datoms, algebraic-effect handlers with audit semantics, homoiconic plan ASTs, transactional shared state, machine-readable boundary specs, and a REPL --- yield derived capabilities (accountability, replay, counterfactual fidelity, composable safety, compositional skill learning, multi-agent coordination) that are consequences of the substrate, not independently-engineered features.

LLM agents in production expose recurring gaps: regulators asking \emph{``what did the agent believe at 14:03, and why?''} get prose-log narratives; post-incident counterfactuals are simulated by prompt perturbation that changes more than the intended variable; self-rewriting skill libraries accumulate drift~\citep{hannecke2026ssgm}; single-layer guardrails cannot compose policy/dry-run/cache/rate-limit/audit. The field offers partial solutions~\citep{kurtic2025zep,cheng2025pangolin,khattab2023dspy,wang2023voyager,anon2026camo} but none are composed: five substrates, five consistency models, five failure modes.

\textbf{Contribution.} We present \textbf{Persistence}, a cognitive-runtime substrate built around a single claim: accountability, replay, counterfactuals, composable safety, and compositional skill learning can be derived from \emph{one} substrate rather than re-engineered per system. v0.1.0a1 + v0.4.0a1 + v0.5.1 ships six of seven modules (\textbf{931 tests green, 7 xfailed}); Plan execution and REPL remain Phase 2.C / 2.E. The substrate is asserted once; each derived capability is a property we check against the log, not a feature engineered per agent. Neural agency operates over an explicitly symbolic substrate: bitemporal datom queries with a Datalog-shaped surface, EDN AST grammars for plans, policy-as-data, and Malli-style specs with an \C{explain\_for\_llm} self-healing surface. We do not claim a Datalog rule engine or Z3-discharged \C{verify} leaves~\citep{fan2024proofofthought} in Phase 1; both are adjacent future work.

\section{Related Work}\label{sec:related}

\textbf{Substrate side.} Agent memory has moved from vector RAG to graph-structured stores with bitemporal edges (Zep~\citep{kurtic2025zep}, Memento~\citep{n1n2026memento}); mutable-memory variants (Mem0, A-Mem) drift without a formal write-gate~\citep{hannecke2026ssgm}. Algebraic-effect dispatch for LLM scripting~\citep{wang2025composable,cheng2025pangolin} stacks interceptors for retry, caching, and observability. None unify temporal memory, effect dispatch, and a formal audit chain into one substrate; Persistence does, treating \emph{every} datom (memory, audit, plan, skill, transaction) as bitemporal so the audit handler wraps the full effect catalog and every run is replayable.

\textbf{Agent-programming side.} DSPy~\citep{khattab2023dspy} compiles declarative programs; LLM+PDDL integrations~\citep{liu2023llmp,valmeekam2023planbench,kambhampati2024canllm} route through classical planners or argue LLMs-as-planners are unsound. The neurosymbolic-reasoning line --- LTN~\citep{badreddine2022ltn}, DeepProbLog~\citep{manhaeve2018deepproblog}, NeuPSL~\citep{pryor2023neupsl} --- integrates at the inference layer rather than the agent-substrate layer we target. Voyager~\citep{wang2023voyager} and Memento-Skills~\citep{memento2026skills} accumulate skill libraries, but promotion-on-first-success generates long tails of single-use skills; Persistence's Plan module enforces a four-gate criterion over versioned, content-addressed skills with parent pointers. For counterfactual replay, CAMO~\citep{anon2026camo}, AgentHER~\citep{anon2026agenther} (extending HER~\citep{andrychowicz2017her}), AgenTracer~\citep{anon2025agentracer}, and Abduct-Act-Predict~\citep{anon2025aap} use simulator-internal alignment or fault-injection; Persistence yields byte-identical trajectory hashes \emph{in the fully-instrumented NO-OP setting} (toy agents with per-step rng-consumption recorded) because immutability + content-addressing makes aligned randomness a substrate property, not a simulator hack. We do not claim that outside.

\section{The Persistence Thesis}\label{sec:thesis}

We state six substrate invariants. Invariants 1, 2, 5 are fully realized in Phase 1; invariant 3 is realized at the shape + parse + validate layer by Phase 2.B (execution Phase 2.C); 4 and 6 are frozen at the spec-registry boundary with runtime in Phase 2.

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Every fact is immutable, temporal, content-addressed.} Memory, audit, plan, skill, transaction are all datoms; provenance, counterfactual branching, audit trails are derived. \emph{[Phase 1: shipped.]}
\item \textbf{Every action is an effect.} LLM calls, tool calls, memory writes, and side effects pass through a composable handler stack; policy, safety, caching, dry-run, retry are handler instances. \emph{[Phase 1: shipped, 15-op catalog.]}
\item \textbf{Every plan is an EDN AST.} Agents read, edit, and evolve their own plans; skills are named AST subtrees promoted under statistical evidence; plan optimization is structural search. \emph{[Phase 2.B v0.2.0a1: parse/walk/spec-validation over the \C{[tag \{attrs\} *children]} AST with 128-bit content-addressing; execution Phase 2.C.]}
\item \textbf{Every shared state change is a transaction.} STM coordinates multi-agent belief updates without locks or message passing. \emph{[Phase 2.D v0.5.1: shipped --- snapshot-isolated dosync (CM + decorator + factory forms) over datom-backed refs; commit gate \C{any\_datoms\_since(t\_start)} guarded by an atomic \C{store.\_lock} window; intent-log read-set emitted into commit-datom provenance; \C{:effect/txn-commit} promoted to a first-class \C{AuditEntry} field threading transactional commits into the audit chain.]}
\item \textbf{Every LLM boundary has a spec.} Parse-don't-validate at trust boundaries; generative testing from specs; self-healing via spec-error feedback. \emph{[Phase 1: shipped, 186 tests.]}
\item \textbf{Everything is REPL-live.} Inspection, editing, rewind, and speculative branching apply to running production agents. \emph{[Rewind subsumed by \C{as\_of}; REPL Phase 2.]}
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Thesis.} Implemented together as a single runtime, these six invariants make accountability, replay, counterfactuals, composable safety, skill promotion, coordinated multi-agency, and live production steering \emph{properties of the substrate} rather than features engineered per agent. Six capabilities ship in v0.5.1 (Phase 1 + 2.B + 2.D); Plan-execution and REPL ship in Phase 2.C / 2.E on the same substrate with no schema changes --- that non-requirement is the testable form of the thesis.

\section{Formalization}\label{sec:formal}

\subsection{Datoms and bitemporal queries}\label{sec:datoms}

A \textbf{datom} is an eight-tuple:

\begin{equation}\label{eq:datom}
d = \langle e,\ a,\ v,\ \tau,\ \tau_{sys},\ \nu_{from},\ \nu_{to},\ \omega \rangle
\end{equation}

where $e$ is an entity, $a$ a namespaced attribute, $v$ an EDN value, $\tau$ a monotonic transaction id, $\tau_{sys}$ the ingestion time, $\nu_{from},\nu_{to}$ the valid-time interval, and $\omega \in \{\text{assert}, \text{retract}\}$. A provenance record $\pi$ ships as the \C{Provenance} \C{TypedDict} (\C{total=False}) over seven known keys --- \C{source}, \C{tx\_time}, \C{handler\_id}, \C{canonical\_call}, \C{parent\_provenance\_hash}, \C{superseded\_by\_tx}, plus a free-form \C{extra} map. The schema is deliberately model-pluggable: \C{canonical\_call} is a content hash of (model, prompt, tools, params); no \C{:provider} field --- the neural model is a plug-in, not embedded in the substrate's shape. Phase 1 seals provenance with SHA-256; per-transaction ed25519 signing is Phase 2 (\sectionref{sec:privacy}). Core queries: $\text{asOf}(D, t) = \{d \mid \tau_{sys}(d) \leq t\}$; $\text{validAsOf}(D, t) = \{d \mid \omega = \text{assert},\, \nu_{from} \leq t < \nu_{to}\}$; $\text{history}(D, e) = \{d \mid \text{entity}(d) = e\}$ sorted by $\tau$; $\text{branch}(D, t, \Delta) = \text{asOf}(D, t) \cup \Delta$.

\begin{proposition}[Branch is a logical operation over the shipped store]\label{prop:branch}
\C{branch(D, t, $\Delta$)} returns a new \C{DB} value backed by a fresh in-memory store seeded with \C{asOf(D, t)} and extended with $\Delta$; writes to the branched value cannot leak back into the parent. \emph{Complexity:} on the Phase 1 \C{InMemoryStore}, $O(|D|)$ seed plus $O(|\Delta|)$ additions. \emph{Phase 2 upgrade:} a persistent HAMT backing store reduces seed to $O(|\Delta| \log |D|)$; the \C{Store} Protocol makes this drop-in.
\end{proposition}

\emph{Remark.} The list-backed Phase-1 complexity is honest rather than ideal; \emph{isolation} holds unconditionally and is the load-bearing property for counterfactual reasoning.

\subsection{Effects, handlers, and well-formedness}\label{sec:effects}

An effect signature is a typed pair $\langle \text{op}: \kappa, \text{args}: \sigma_{in} \rightharpoonup \sigma_{out} \rangle$. The minimum catalog $K$ comprises 15 ops: \C{llm/call}, \C{tool/call}, \C{mem/read}, \C{mem/write}, \C{decide}, \C{ask-user}, \C{emit-artifact}, \C{sleep}, \C{random}, \C{env/read}, \C{net/fetch}, \C{secret/use}, \C{cost/charge}, \C{clock/now}, \C{audit/emit}. A \textbf{handler} $h$ is a partial function $(\kappa, \sigma_{in}, k: \sigma_{out} \to \alpha, \mathrm{ctx}) \to \alpha$ with continuation semantics. A stack $H = [h_1, \dots, h_n]$ ($h_1$ outermost) dispatches $\kappa$ to the outermost handler whose domain contains it; that handler invokes the continuation, delegating to the remaining stack.

\begin{proposition}[Well-formedness; machine-checkable on the shipped runtime]\label{prop:wellformed}
A stack $H$ over catalog $K$ is well-formed iff for every $\kappa \in K$, at least one handler above the raw base handles $\kappa$. The shipped \C{Runtime.is\_well\_formed(catalog)} (\C{src/persistence/effect/runtime.py}) decides this property in $O(|H| \cdot |K|)$ time --- \emph{linear in stack size for fixed catalog} ($|K|=15$ in the shipped runtime); \C{Runtime.uncovered\_ops(catalog)} returns the witness set. At runtime, \C{Runtime.perform(op, \dots)} raises \C{Unhandled} when no handler covers $\kappa$ --- the property is not merely asserted but \emph{enforced on every call}.
\end{proposition}

This is the paper's strongest formal contribution on the Phase-1 artifact: a decidable, linear-time, machine-checked completeness property with runtime enforcement via \C{Unhandled}. Properties above (audit-chain integrity \sectionref{sec:audit}, replay determinism \sectionref{sec:replay}) build on well-formedness. Handlers compose \emph{masked} (Koka semantics): $\text{mask}_a(h)$ prevents $h$ from intercepting operations of attribute $a$ within its own body, eliminating re-entrant loops.

\subsection{The Merkle-hashed audit chain}\label{sec:audit}

When the \C{audit} handler wraps a set of operations $W \subseteq K$, each effect on $\kappa \in W$ emits a datom recording \C{(op, args, verdict, provenance, prev\_hash)} before the continuation fires. Each entry's \C{id} is computed as \C{sha256(canonical(fields \textbackslash{} \{id\}))} --- i.e.\ the canonical serialization of every entry field except the \C{id} itself, including \C{prev\_hash}; chaining is enforced by setting \C{prev\_hash := previous.id} (Proposition~\ref{prop:tamper}). Phase 1 ships \C{make\_audit\_handler(wraps, \dots)} (default \C{("llm/call",)}), \C{verify\_chain(entries) $\to$ bool} (re-derives \C{prev\_hash}; detects field-mutation tamper), and \C{audit\_entry\_to\_datom} flowing audit entries into the Fact log. As of v0.4.0a1, \C{audit\_entry\_to\_datom} also emits a \C{parent\_provenance\_hash} alias alongside \C{:prev-hash}, bridging audit-entry datoms into the typed \C{Provenance} schema so \C{DB.causal\_history(e)} walks audit chains and fact-log derivation chains under one query primitive. Authenticity (\emph{who} signed) is distinct from integrity and not claimed for Phase 1; per-transaction ed25519 is Phase 2 (\sectionref{sec:privacy}).

\begin{proposition}[Audit-chain immutability]\label{prop:tamper}
For any audit-chain $C = \langle e_0, \dots, e_n \rangle$ produced by \C{make\_audit\_handler}, \C{verify\_chain(C)} returns true iff for all $i$, $e_i.\text{id} = \text{sha256}(\text{canonical}(e_i.\text{fields} \setminus \{\text{id}\}))$ and $e_i.\text{prev\_hash} = e_{i-1}.\text{id}$ --- i.e. no entry has been mutated, deleted, reordered, or truncated from the middle. Exercised end-to-end by \C{tests/effect/test\_audit.py} (tampering / deletion / reordering tests). Tail-truncation is allowed by construction and must be detected by regulators comparing a separately-recorded expected length.
\end{proposition}

\subsection{Plans (Phase 2.B shipped: parse/walk/spec-validation; execution Phase 2.C)}\label{sec:plans}

A plan is a labeled tree: internal nodes are \emph{control operators} ($\text{seq}$, $\text{par}$, $\text{choice}$, $\text{loop}$, $\text{race}$, $\text{let}$, $\text{branch}$); leaves are \emph{effect invocations} ($\text{tool-call}$, $\text{llm-call}$, $\text{code}$, $\text{checkpoint}$) or \emph{cognitive operators} ($\text{reflect}$, $\text{verify}$, $\text{call-skill}$). Every node $n$ carries content identity $\text{id}(n) = \text{sha256}(\text{canonical}(n))[:32]$ as its Merkle-DAG handle. The \C{:persistence.plan/node} spec is registered in Phase 1; the Phase 2.B \C{persistence.plan} module (v0.2.0a1) ships parse, unparse, depth-first walk, and spec validation against this shape. Encoding nodes as EDN vectors \C{[:tag \{attrs\} \& children]} rather than maps makes plan ASTs \emph{homoiconic}: the homoiconicity contract's allowed self-edits (read, splice, compose, rewrite, fork, promote) reduce to list-splicing over well-typed vectors.

\begin{proposition}[Plan content-addressing with descendant propagation]\label{prop:plan}
For any plan node $n = \langle \text{tag}, \text{attrs}, \text{children} \rangle$ constructed through the shipped \C{Node} constructor, $\text{id}(n) = \text{sha256}(\text{canonical}(n)).\text{hexdigest}()[{:}32]$ (i.e.\ the first 32 hex characters $=$ 128-bit prefix of the SHA-256 digest) over $n$'s tag, alphabetically-sorted attr map (non-finite floats rejected; non-string / colon-prefixed / empty / reserved \C{id} keys rejected at both construct and parse as defense-in-depth against EDN hash-poisoning), and its children's ids recursively. Hence for any descendant modification at depth $d$ producing $T'$, every ancestor id on the root-to-leaf path differs between $T$ and $T'$. Property-checked at \C{max\_examples=200} on round-trip id preservation and descendant-mutation propagation over all 16 node kinds. Spec conformance against \C{:persistence.plan/node} is enforced at parse when \C{strict=True}. The 7 \C{xfail} cases are tightening tests for Phase-2.C semantics (per-kind required-attr enforcement, e.g.\ \C{:loop} cardinality bounds, \C{:branch} exhaustiveness, \C{:case} disjointness); the shipped claim covers parse / walk / spec-validation / content-addressing only --- \emph{not} execution semantics --- so the xfails do not mask any property machine-checked above. Birthday-collision probability at 1\% reaches $\approx 2.6 \times 10^{18}$ plans at 128 bits, argued in the corresponding ship commit's CHANGELOG.
\end{proposition}

\emph{Neurosymbolic reading.} Prop.~\ref{prop:plan} is the identity law for a homoiconic NeSy AST: skeleton symbolic (editable, optimizable, regulator-readable), leaves (\C{:llm-call}, \C{:code}, \C{:reflect}) neural. Descendant propagation makes every structural edit produce a new ancestor-id path --- the surface Phase-2.C optimizers (MIPROv2, MCTS-over-Plan) walk; the contract forbids edits to \C{:verify} nodes (would defeat Proof-of-Thought~\citep{fan2024proofofthought}). The v0.4.0a1 \C{Dispatcher} adds handler-per-tag dispatch over the AST walker (\C{\_walk.py}); \C{PLAN\_CANONICAL\_VERSION} stays at 1.

\subsection{Trajectories and replay}\label{sec:replay}

A \textbf{trajectory} is an ordered sequence of effect datoms sharing a run-id, plus a seed vector $\sigma = \langle \sigma_{llm}, \sigma_{tool}, \sigma_{env} \rangle$. An \textbf{intervention set} $I = [\langle \text{step}, \text{field}, \text{new-value} \rangle, \dots]$ is a list of per-step modifications, sorted by step. The replay operator copies $T_i$ verbatim for $i < I.\text{step}$, applies the intervention at $I.\text{step}$, and re-executes the suffix with effect calls routed through a \emph{replay handler} that returns cached responses keyed by $\langle \text{step-index}, \text{op}, \text{args-hash} \rangle$ (full cache model and trajectory hash given in Proposition~\ref{prop:noop}).

\begin{proposition}[Replay determinism --- stronger NO-OP form]\label{prop:noop}
If all non-determinism in the agent routes through effects in catalog $K$ \textbf{and} the per-step rng-consumption pattern is recorded, $\text{replay}(T, I)$ produces a deterministic counterfactual trajectory that diverges from $T$ exactly at $I.\text{step}$. \emph{Cache model:} the replay handler keys cached responses by $\langle \text{step-index}, \text{op}, \text{args-hash} \rangle$ rather than args-hash alone, so that two distinct steps issuing the same op with identical args still resolve to their respective recorded outcomes; the RNG-consumption pattern is what guarantees the cache key sequence reproduces deterministically. \emph{Trajectory hash:} $\text{trajectory\_hash}(T) = \text{sha256}(\text{canonical\_dumps}(\langle e_{\text{run-id}}, \text{op}, \text{args}, \text{value}, \tau, \text{prev-hash} \rangle_{i=0..n}))$ over the effect-datom sequence; it does not include wall-clock or non-recorded environment reads. Phase 1 strengthens the proposition for the NO-OP case: for an intervention that applies but changes no value, $\text{trajectory\_hash}(\text{replay}(T, I_{\text{noop}})) = \text{trajectory\_hash}(T)$ --- \emph{byte-identical} on the canonical serialization, not merely statistically close. Verified by \C{tests/replay/test\_determinism.py} (NO-OP byte-identity test) on toy agents with fixed per-step rng-consumption; LLM-leaf generalization is Phase 2 (\sectionref{sec:eval}).
\end{proposition}

This strengthens CAMO's aligned-randomness on a structural canonical-hash axis rather than a statistical outcome-delta one; LLM-leaf generalization requires per-step rng-state recording (Phase 2).

\subsection{Transactions and specs}\label{sec:transactions}

\textbf{Refs and \C{dosync}.}\label{sec:transactions-refs} A \textbf{ref} is a pair $\langle \iota, \tau_{\text{current}} \rangle$. A transaction body is a thunk that reads and writes refs; commit emits an atomic set of datoms, CAS-ing on all read refs' $\tau$. On conflict the transaction is retried; composition is automatic. The Txn-module runtime ships in Phase 2.D (v0.5.1: snapshot-isolated \C{dosync} with read-set + intent-log provenance and the \C{:effect/txn-commit} audit-field hook; \sectionref{sec:impl-txn}). Multi-process Postgres \texttt{SERIALIZABLE} is deferred to v0.6.x.

\textbf{Specs and the self-healing LLM contract.}\label{sec:specs}

A \textbf{spec} is a predicate-generator pair; conforming yields \C{Conformed} \textbar{} \C{ConformError} (no silent coercion), so business logic types against conformed values and raw inputs cannot reach it. Specs are first-class EDN --- composable, diffable, versionable. \textbf{Self-healing contract (shipped):} on conform failure, \C{spec.explain\_for\_llm(err)} returns a structured field-path / reason / Fix-annotated hint, so when an LLM emits a malformed tool call the spec layer returns a prompt-ready correction the agent retries against --- a \textbf{conform $\to$ explain $\to$ retry} loop where the symbolic layer emits hints the neural layer consumes, a concrete neurosymbolic contribution distinct from post-hoc error logging. \textbf{Self-conforming producers (shipped):} every load-bearing producer calls \C{spec.conform} on return and raises on mismatch, so defects fail at the producer's site, not inside a consumer that read a bad wire form.

\textbf{Forward-compatible spec-first commitment.} The two plan specs were registered in Phase 1 \emph{before} the Plan module existed --- a parse-don't-validate methodology that locks the data shape before code depends on it.

\section{Implementation}\label{sec:impl}

Persistence is implemented across seven modules. The reference implementation is in Python for integration parity with existing agent stacks. Phase 1 ships four modules (Fact, Effect, Spec, Replay); Phase 2.B ships Plan (minimal: parse / unparse / walk / spec-validation); Phase 2.D ships Txn (snapshot-isolated STM); Phase 2.C and 2.E remain (Plan execution; REPL).

\subsection{Fact (Phase 1 --- shipped)}\label{sec:impl-fact}

Fact ships a \C{Store} Protocol with \C{InMemoryStore} and \C{SQLiteStore} backends (Postgres adapter and Zstd segment compression Phase 2; Datomic Index Model~\citealp{tonsky2023datomic}). Writes pass through a transactor computing auto-retractions for cardinality-one overwrites; \C{Store.allocate\_and\_append} runs \C{MAX(tx)+1} and row inserts inside a single atomic transaction (SQLite \C{BEGIN IMMEDIATE}; in-memory \C{threading.Lock}), closing the TOCTOU window a split allocator would leave open --- the concurrency invariant Proposition~\ref{prop:noop} implicitly depends on (16 threads $\times$ 50 transacts $\to$ 800 unique tx ids, zero collisions). Projection (\C{ProjectionAdapter}, \C{DictProjection}) gains \C{fork(branch\_id)} + \C{rebuild()} (v0.4.0a1) as the projection-side counterpart to \C{branch()}; \C{DB.causal\_history(e, max\_depth=16)} ships as the lineage primitive.

\subsection{Effect (Phase 1 --- shipped)}\label{sec:impl-effect}

Effect implements a handler-stack runtime; handlers are EDN-declared, the canonical regulated stack is \C{audit $\to$ policy $\to$ dry-run $\to$ cache $\to$ retry $\to$ rate-limit $\to$ raw}. The \C{audit} handler emits a datom into Fact chained via \C{prev-hash} (Proposition~\ref{prop:tamper}); policies are EDN evaluated by a $\sim$200-line evaluator supporting principal attributes, op-matching, and conditional effects. \C{Runtime.is\_well\_formed} and \C{Runtime.uncovered\_ops} are public API (Proposition~\ref{prop:wellformed}).

\subsection{Spec (Phase 1 --- shipped)}\label{sec:impl-spec}

Spec provides Malli-equivalent predicate-generator pairs over EDN data; ten canonical specs ship across \C{persistence.\{fact, effect, replay, plan\}} (the two plan specs registered before the Plan module). Failed conforms emit \C{spec.explain\_for\_llm} self-healing hints.

\subsection{Replay (Phase 1 --- shipped)}\label{sec:impl-replay}

Replay records trajectories as effect-datom sequences and intercepts effects via a replay handler returning cached responses keyed by $\langle \text{step-index}, \text{op}, \text{args-hash} \rangle$ (Proposition~\ref{prop:noop}). \C{gen\_regression\_test(trajectory)} emits a pytest-source snapshot-test against the loaded trajectory (snapshot-replay, not agent-re-run; full regression re-execution is Phase 2). DPO-pair extraction is staged on per-step rng-recording (Phase 2).

\subsection{Plan (Phase 2.B --- shipped; Phase 2.C --- designed)}\label{sec:impl-plan}

Plan v0.2.0a1 ships parse / unparse / walk / spec-validation over the uniform \C{[tag \{attrs\} *children]} EDN AST across 16 node kinds, with a frozen-slots \C{Node} dataclass whose content-addressed \C{id} is the proposition target (Proposition~\ref{prop:plan}; 172 plan-module tests, full-repo suite \textbf{931 passed + 7 xfailed} v0.5.1). The 7 \C{xfail} cases are tightening tests for Phase-2.C semantics (\C{:loop} cardinality, \C{:branch} exhaustiveness, \C{:case} disjointness); the shipped claim covers parse / walk / spec-validation / content-addressing only --- not execution semantics. Phase 2.C: \C{fold} primitive, OpenTelemetry-spanned evaluator, optimizer ladder (MIPROv2 + UCT-MCTS + topology search), 4-gate skill promotion.

\subsection{Txn (Phase 2.D --- shipped, v0.5.1)}\label{sec:impl-txn}

Txn implements snapshot-isolated STM over refs backed by Fact: commits check \C{any\_datoms\_since(t\_start)} against the read-set inside an atomic \C{store.\_lock} window (TOCTOU-free), retry via the \C{dosync} loop, and emit a tx-commit datom. Three surface forms ship: \C{with db.dosync()} (CM), \C{@db.dosync(max\_retries=N)} (decorator), and \C{db.dosync()(callable)} (factory). Read-set + intent-log thread into commit-datom provenance (v0.5.1 N1); per-ref attribute specs route through \C{Ref.spec\_attr} (N3); \C{:effect/txn-commit} is a first-class \C{AuditEntry} field (N2). Commit semantics follow the Multiverse~\citep{bieniusa2026multiverse} hybrid; multi-process coordination via Postgres \texttt{SERIALIZABLE} is deferred to v0.6.x.

\subsection{REPL (Phase 2 --- designed)}\label{sec:impl-repl}

REPL will expose a capability-gated WebSocket extending a thread-routing Claude Code client; rewind resumes from an earlier $\tau$ via the as-of view, branch runs a speculative path in a sandboxed dry-run-forced handler stack, and writes to the authoritative fact store require signed operator approval.

\section{Discussion}\label{sec:discussion}

\textbf{Limitations.} Write latency rises by a constant per-transaction overhead from transactor validation and auto-retraction; figures on \C{InMemoryStore}/\C{SQLiteStore} are [TBD] for the camera-ready. Append-only storage is $\approx 4\times$ a mutable-memory baseline, partially offset by content-addressing and Phase-2 Zstd compression. \C{branch} is $O(|D|)$-copy (Proposition~\ref{prop:branch}); HAMT backing is the Phase 2 upgrade. Replay byte-identity holds only for toy agents (Proposition~\ref{prop:noop}); LLM-trajectory generalization requires per-step rng-state recording (Phase 2). Audit universality is a stack-configuration contract, not a substrate invariant.

\textbf{Privacy architecture.} The authoritative datom log runs on operator-controlled infrastructure; projections are regenerable. Phase 1 ships chain \emph{integrity} (SHA-256 + \C{verify\_chain}); cryptographic \emph{authenticity} via per-transaction ed25519 is Phase 2. \C{:privacy :local} on \C{:llm-call} routes to local models (Qwen, DeepSeek, Llama); \C{:visibility :private} on skills enforces never-to-cloud; telemetry exits to an operator-controlled OpenTelemetry collector with no SaaS egress.

\textbf{Adoption path.} An existing agent stack migrates additively: (1) stand up Fact alongside the memory system with a datom-emitting write interceptor; (2) backfill from mutable state with \C{tx-time}/\C{valid-from} := \C{created\_at}; (3) build \C{as\_of}/\C{history}/\C{branch} as thin functions over the log. Effect, Spec, Replay migrate one at a time; Plan, Txn, REPL land in Phase 2.

\textbf{Relation to neurosymbolic AI.} The substrate is symbolic, agency is neural, and they couple through three concrete contracts beyond storage-and-audit: spec-driven self-healing (\sectionref{sec:specs}), plan-as-data with content identity (\sectionref{sec:plans}), and replay determinism over neural leaves (\sectionref{sec:replay}). The shipped substrate carries accountability, composition, and formal guarantees; the neural layer carries expressiveness. This positions Persistence between pure-neural (no guarantees) and pure-symbolic (no expressiveness) approaches as a candidate substrate for neurosymbolic systems beyond LLM agents.

\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}

We presented Persistence, a cognitive-runtime substrate for accountable neurosymbolic agents. v0.1.0a1 + v0.4.0a1 + v0.5.1 (\textbf{931 tests green + 7 xfailed}) ships six modules (Fact, Effect, Spec, Replay, Plan-minimal, Txn) realizing the six substrate-derived capabilities of \sectionref{sec:thesis}; five formal propositions are stated and machine-checked on the shipped artifact, including the headline pair: well-formedness decidable in linear time and enforced at runtime via \C{Unhandled}; NO-OP replay producing a canonical-hash byte-identical to the factual trajectory's (toy agents, recorded per-step rng-consumption). Plan-execution and REPL ship in Phase 2.C / 2.E. The core claim is that accountability, replay, composable safety, skill learning, multi-agent coordination, and live steering derive from \emph{one} substrate rather than six --- properties of the log, checkable against the artifact, not features engineered per agent.

\textbf{Future work.} (1) A persistent-trie \C{Store} making Proposition~\ref{prop:branch}'s $O(\log n)$ branch shipped; (2) per-step rng-state recording unblocking byte-identical LLM-trajectory replay; (3) ed25519 per-transaction signing; (4) Z3-discharged \C{verify} leaves following Proof-of-Thought~\citep{fan2024proofofthought}.
